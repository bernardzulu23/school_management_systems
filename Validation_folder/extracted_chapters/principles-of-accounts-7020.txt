1 
Construct  
Demonstrates strong accounting competences, financial literacy and ethical decision-making skills and 
applies them responsibly in real business and non-business contexts. 
2 
Elements of Construct (EoC) 
The Principles of Accounts Examination will be based on the four (4) Elements of Construct and they will 
be as follows: 
 EoC 1 Demonstrates sound accounting decisions based on key accounting principles or concepts, 
procedures and ethical standards 
 Applies key accounting principles or concepts, procedures and ethical standards to make 
well-reasoned, transparent and responsible accounting decisions. 
 
Demonstrates procedural precision and ethical integrity in finance and accounting. 
 
Exhibits regulatory compliance and professional judgement in accounting. 
 
Appreciates the accounting career prospects.  
EoC 2 
Demonstrates proficiency in recording business transactions in ledger accounts 
 
Applies understanding of source documents in the recording of business transactions. 
 
Demonstrates understanding of manual and electronic accounting systems. 
 
Demonstrates accurate recording of business transactions in the books of prime entry. 
 
Transfers appropriately journal amounts to ledger accounts. 
 
Demonstrates accurate preparation of ledger accounts through effective application of double 
entry book keeping system. 
 
Interprets performance of ledger accounts to facilitate preparation of financial statements. 
EoC 3 
Analyses the effective implementation of reconciliation and verification tools in accounting 
 
Demonstrates mastery in the use of verification tools for identification and correction of 
errors and discrepancies. 
 
Demonstrates mastery of reconciliation processes involving cash book, bank statements, 
customer and supplier transactions. 
 
Performs verifications and reconciliations effectively to ensure accuracy,   
reliability and integrity in accounting records. 
EoC 4 
Prepares and analyses financial statements 
 
Prepares financial statements to measure performance of sole proprietorships, partnerships, 
non-profit organisations and manufacturing businesses. 
 
Organises single/incomplete records into usable accounts for preparation of financial 
statements and analysis. 
 Performs financial analysis to identify operational deficiencies, risks and makes reliable 
forecasts for timely and informed decisions. 
 
Applies analysis of accounting ratios appropriately to interpret and evaluate business 
performance.

3.1. 
Structure 
The structure for Principles of Accounts Examination shall comprise of SBA and Final Examination 
as follows; 
3.1.1 School Based Assessment - Component 1 
SBA tasks will be chosen, administered and marked by the teacher at school level only in Form 
2 and Form 3 using different types of formative and summative tasks.  Examples include class 
exercises, home works, quizzes, assignments, case studies, excursions and experiential activities, 
presentations and tests.  A total of twelve (12) SBA tasks will be administered, six (6) in Form 2 
and six (6) again in Form 3. The teacher should endeavour to achieve contextual and practical 
balance ensuring holistic acquisition and application of the cognitive accounting knowledge, 
accounting computational psychomotor skills and accounting values and ethical attitudes to be 
demonstrated by the learner. The tasks will comprise of real-life accounting scenarios and 
situations to which the learner will be required to fully apply critical thinking regarding 
accounting principles and ethical standards to provide solutions.   
The structure and type of each SBA task will be determined by the teacher at school level.  
3.1.2 Final Examination Component 2 
The Final Examination (FE) Component 2 will be centrally set and marked by the 
Examinations Council of Zambia (ECZ) and will be administered at the end of Form 4 
according to the Final Examination time table. 
The Final Examination Principles of Accounts Paper will consist of two sections namely 
Section A and Section B.  
Section A will have three (3) compulsory items based on EoC 1, EoC 2 and EoC 3, While 
Section B will have two (2) optional items both from EoC 4, from which candidates will 
choose any one (1) item. Section B will focus on preparation and analysis of financial 
statements which will have more marks due to more competences. Items will have the real 
financial and accounting problem, scenario or situation, financial data extracts among others 
upon which candidates will demonstrate acquired competences based on the three domains of 
cognitive accounting knowledge, the psychomotor accounting skills and the affective 
accounting values and attitudes. The items will be based on the four (4) Elements of Construct

          3.2     Test Content 
Assessment will be based on the CDC 2024 Principles of Accounts Syllabus, ECZ developed 
Assessment Scheme, Test Framework and Test Specification. Skills assessed will include 
knowledge, understanding, application, analysis, ethical thinking and problem solving. 
3.2.1 Test Item or question Types 
 (i)     School Based Assessment Component 1  
 
 
SBA will consist of structural tasks in form of formative and summative assessments 
such as   class exercises, quizzes, homework, assignments, projects, research, report 
writing and presentations and summative tests. The teacher shall choose, administer and 
mark the tasks at school level. The teacher is advised to ensure holistic learner 
development through diversity of question types to adequately demonstrate the cognitive, 
psychomotor and affective domains. Computational, application, analytical, evaluative 
and investigative question types among others will be used.  
  (ii) Final Examination - Component 2  
 The Final Examination (FE) Component 2 will comprise of subjective semi and 
structured item types which will be attached to real-life accounting scenarios or 
situations. All the items will be scenario based which will require candidates to solve 
identified accounting problems. Candidates will be required to apply strong accounting 
competences of real-life accounting application, interpretation, analysis and evaluation. 
Items will require candidates to exhibit key and specific competences, apply them to 
solve problems and interpret correctly to give decisive accounting decisions.  
3.2.2     Level of Difficulty  
The level of difficulty for SBA tasks and Final Examination test items will be influenced by 
the   following main factors among others:  
 
(i) Content complexity referring to the depth of the subject mastery and extent of knowledge 
to be demonstrated by learners or candidates. 
 
(ii) Scenario or question complexity referring to the mental processes required to clearly 
understand the problem or gaps the question poses and present accurate solutions 
 
(iii) Scenario type may be practical, knowledge, procedural, analytical and ethical based.

the skill, knowledge, altitudes and values. 
 
(v)  Level of autonomy where the lower the dependence the higher the difficulty and vice

(vi) Format and authenticity complexity referring to the type of task or item and the real life

(vii) cognitive complexity referring to the level of thinking demanded based on the Revised

Group Project Task 
In groups of 5, you are required to identify a particular business activity of your choice. Track its transactions 
and keep complete accounting records within a period of four (4) months.  
You must prepare: source documents, books of original entry, ledgers, a trial balance, and full financial 
statements and interpretations.  
In the final stage, analyse the performance of the business using accounting ratios and provide a written 
project report with recommendations. This project carries a total of 50 marks.‖ 
Project instructions and phases 
Month 1: Business Setup and Source Documents (5 marks) 
 
Select a readily available business activity (e.g tuck shop, fruit stand, stationery sales). 
 
Document transactions and prepare source documents (e.g receipts, invoices, cash slips). 
 
Record transactions in books of original entry (e.g cash book, sales journal, purchases journal). 
Month 2: Ledger Posting and Trial Balance (10 marks) 
 
Post transactions into ledgers. 
 
Balance accounts. 
 
Prepare a trial balance at the end of the month. 
Month 3: Financial Statements Preparation (10 marks) 
 
Prepare the Trading, Profit & Loss Account. 
 
Prepare the Balance Sheet. 
 
Ensure accuracy, proper format, and compliance with accounting principles. 
Month 4: Analysis and Interpretation (5 marks) 
 
Calculate key ratios (gross profit %, net profit %, current ratio, return on capital). 
 
Interpret results in a written report. 
 
Suggest practical improvements for the business.

6.2 Exemplar Items for Final Examination 
EoC 1: Demonstrates sound accounting decisions based on key accounting principles or concepts, procedures and 
ethical standards. 
Specific Competence: Applies key accounting principles or concepts, procedures and ethical standards to make well-
reasoned, transparent and responsible accounting decisions. 
Item 1 
Mula Bakery, a sole proprietor, plans to apply for a K750,000.00 loan from FN Commercial Bank to finance the 
purchase of an automated high-volume oven and a modern dough mixer. As part of the loan evaluation process, the bank 
requires audited Financial Statements to determine the bakery‘s creditworthiness as a basis for granting a loan. 
However, Mula Bakery has concerns that the loan may not be approved due to an outstanding overdue liability of 
K250,000.00 owed to ZYX Limited. In an attempt to appear financially stronger, the bakery has approached its external 
auditor (a registered member of Zambia Institute of Chartered Accountants – ZICA) requesting approval of Financial 
Statements that exclude the K250,000.00 liability. 
 
Task 1  
As a learner of Principles of Accounts, what advice would you give to the external auditor? Provide three ethical 
principles that would guide your advice. (4 marks) 
Task 2  
Advise Mula Bakery on how the prudence and accruals concepts would affect the business financial position when the 
overdue liability is omitted from the Financial Statements. (10 marks) 
Task 3  
As a Compliant Officer from ZICA explain to the Auditor what professional requirements would be violated if the 
Auditor: 
(i)    agreed to hide K250,000.00 liability. 
(ii)  omitted the K250 000 liability.  (2 marks) 
Task 4  
Advise the auditor on the consequences that they may face for failing to adhere to ethical standards in this scenario. (2 
marks) 
Task 5  
As a learner of Principles of Accounts, you are tasked to give a talk to your fellow learners on the possible effects that 
the omission of the liability from the Financial Statements would cause on the bank and stakeholders. (2 marks) 
 
       [Total: 20 marks] 
SOLUTION 
Task 1 
Answer  
 
It is unethical to omit the liability because financial statements must reflect the true financial position. 
 
Ethical principles: 
1. Integrity – requires honesty and truthfulness in reporting. 
2. Objectivity – decisions should not be influenced by pressure from the client. (4 marks) 
Task 2 
Answer: 
Advice to Mula Bakery 
Omitting the overdue liability of K250,000.00 from the financial statements would violate both the Prudence Concept 
and the Accruals Concept, and this would negatively affect the reliability of the financial statements. 
1. Effect in relation to the Prudence Concept 
 
The Prudence Concept requires that liabilities and expenses must not be understated, and assets or profits must 
not be overstated. 
 
If the overdue liability is omitted, the bakery‘s financial statements will show a stronger financial position than 

--- PDF_PAGE 310 ---
- 294 - 
 
it actually has, which is misleading. 
 
This will overstate profit and equity, making the business appear more financially healthy than it really is. 
Advice: The business should include all liabilities, even overdue ones, to present a true and fair view. Being transparent 
protects credibility and avoids ethical and legal consequences.   
Effect in relation to the Accruals Concept 
 
The Accruals Concept states that expenses should be recorded when they are incurred, not when they are paid. 
 
By omitting the K250,000.00 liability, the business would be ignoring an expense that has already been 
incurred. 
 
This will result in understated liabilities and expenses, and overstated profit for the period. 
Advice: The overdue amount must be recognized in the accounts to ensure that financial statements reflect actual 
financial obligations of the business. This gives stakeholders a realistic basis for decision-making. 
Overall Recommendation 
Mula Bakery should not omit the overdue liability. Including it will ensure compliance with key accounting principles, 
maintain professional integrity, and avoid misleading financial statement users such as lenders and investors. Ensuring 
accuracy builds trust and increases long-term sustainability, even if it reduces the chance of loan approval in the short 
term.   (10 marks) 
Task 3 
Answer: 
 
Violation of Code of Professional Conduct under ZICA requiring integrity, objectivity, and professional 
behaviour. 
 
Breach of obligation to prepare true and fair financial statements in accordance with IFRS and ZICA guidelines.  
(2 marks) 
Task 4 
Answer: 
 
Disciplinary action by ZICA (suspension, penalties, deregistration). 
 
Loss of professional reputation and legal consequences if fraud is discovered. (2 marks) 
Task 5  
Answer: 
 
The bank may grant a loan based on misleading financial statements, exposing it to credit risk. 
 
Users make wrong financial and investment decisions due to unreliable information. (2 marks) 
Level of Difficulty: (Understanding, applying, analysing and evaluating 
Key Competences: (Financial education, analytical and critical thinking, citizenship and entrepreneurship)

Chapter 29: Access Arrangements 
 
1 
Background 
It is crucial that assessment practices of learners with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND) 
are guided by sound principles that are fair and inclusive for the learners they are designed for. This 
document aims to provide guidance to teachers, assessors and administrators across institutions on the 
conduct of fair and inclusive assessment.  In inclusive assessment, the Test designs, methods and criteria 
used in the conduct and administration of assessment shape the perception of assessment among learners 
with SEND.  
Assessment of these learners should be grounded on the same educational principles of Competence-
Based curriculum to reflect equity and to give full participation to all learners. Assessment can be 
conducted in a formative and summative way in order to determine the acquisition of knowledge and skills 
of learners.  
In order to create an inclusive assessment environment that enables all learners to perform to their 
potential, access arrangements or reasonable accommodations are given to support those candidates with 
limitations to accessing assessment either summative or formative.  
2 
Purpose 
The purpose of giving Access Arrangements to learners with SEND at Examination for Certificate of 
Secondary Education Ordinary Level (ECSEOL) is to eliminate the unnecessary barriers to standard 
assessments and aims to promote fairness in order to maintain the integrity of the qualification.  
3 
Access Arrangement Provisions 
Access arrangements are not automatic - they must be supported by evidence of need and reflect the 
person‘s normal way of working. The boundaries and parameters within which the Access Arrangements 
shall apply include the following: 
3.1 
Equal opportunities: Access Arrangement aims to provide learners with special educational needs 
an opportunity to access examinations or assessments without any barriers.  
3.2 
Merit-Based Selection: candidates with Special Educational Needs (SEND) will be selected based 
on merit and ability.  
3.3 
Positive discrimination: this entails making reasonable adjustments designed to focus on disabling 
conditions and remove barriers that may prevent full participation and access to examinations by 
learners with SEND.  
3.4 
Evidence-based: Using research-based and proven access arrangements in assessment 
4 
Assessment Objectivity 
Access arrangements must not give the candidate an unfair advantage over others. Adherence to 
examinations regulations: any teacher or lecturer or education administrator who helps candidates with 
access arrangements, for example, scribes or human readers shall strictly adhere to the Examinations 
Guidelines and Regulations. 
5 
Pre-Examination Access Arrangements  
Examination centres must request provisions of modified question papers in advance based on their needs. 
Pre-examination access arrangements are modifications made to the examination question papers that 
involve altering the content, context, or format of existing assessment instruments to better suit the needs 

--- PDF_PAGE 322 ---
- 306 - 
 
of specific learners or to improve their quality and accuracy without necessarily changing the fundamental 
learning objectives being assessed. Pre-examination modifications include Braille and Enlarged Print 
version for candidates with visual impairment and Adapted English question papers for candidates with 
hearing impairment. 
6 
Other Access Arrangements  
Other Access Arrangements given to candidates are applied during the conduct and administration of 
examinations at school level. These include the following: 
6.1 
Extra time 
Candidates with SEND are granted 25 percent more time. Candidates with Special Educational 
Needs are eligible for 25% (percent) extra writing time during examinations. 
6.2 
Intermittent Breaks  
Intermittent breaks occur at irregular intervals whenever a candidate wishes to rest during 
examinations, and a Stop Watch is used for time management. Additionally, some candidates may 
also require breaks during the examination session.  
6.3 
Separate room 
To minimise distractions, some candidates may be allowed to sit their examination in a separate 
room. Candidates who are granted extra time should typically sit their examination in a separate 
room to further reduce distractions. 
6.4 
A Scribe 
Candidates with a disability or injury that affects their ability to write legibly are should be allowed 
to have a human scribe to assist them in completing their written answers. Arrangements can be 
made for a scribe to write answers on behalf of the candidate when writing is challenging. 
6.5 
A Reader 
A candidate with visual impairments or a disability that hinders their ability to read may use a 
human reader. Arrangements should be made to have question papers read to candidates who face 
challenges with reading. 
6.6 
Prompter 
Candidates who face emotional challenges may struggle with concentration and attention span and 
require support to follow instructions and remain focused during tasks. A prompter may assist these 
Candidates.  
6.7 
Tape Recording Assistant  
The candidates under this access arrangement have the provision to have their responses recorded 
on tape. An assistant will be provided to carry out the recording of a candidate's responses on tape. 
6.8 
Transcriber  
Candidates under this Access Arrangement will be assisted by having a written Braille converted to 
ink print. 
7 
Samples of Modified Examination items  for Learners With SEND 
Learners with Hearing Impairment will have their Test Items modified for the purpose of making them be 
simplified to avoid them failing to understand the Items as a result of the questions that may have 
unfamiliar words to them.

Chapter 30: Grading and Certification 
1 
Introduction  
The Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level (ECSEOL) syllabi cover the 
secondary school ordinary level material, namely, Form I, Form II, Form III, and Form IV. The 
assessments during and at the end of the course of study are aimed at evaluating the extent to which 
learners can demonstrate the required competences and apply knowledge, understanding, and skills to 
solve problems in real-life situations. The next section, therefore, describes the grading and certification 
criteria at this level of education and assessment. 
2.  
Grading and Certification 
2.1 
Grading at Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level 
At this level of assessment, components from the school-based assessment and the centrally 
set examination are combined to come up with the final score. By means of a procedure 
called standards fixing and awards, cut scores are agreed on from one examination session 
to another per subject.  
There are five proficiency levels at this level of examination, namely, Outstanding, 
Advanced, Basic, Satisfactory, and Unsatisfactory. Table 1 outlines the competency levels 
and their respective score ranges and descriptors.
