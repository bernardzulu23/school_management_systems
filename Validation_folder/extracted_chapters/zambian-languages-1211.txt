1. 
Construct 
Demonstrates proficient use of listening, speaking, reading and writing skills in real-life situations, 
adapting to audience and purpose to communicate effectively and support life long learning. 
2.  
Elements of Construct 
The Zambian Languages Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level will be 
based on four Elements of Construct as follows: 
EoC 1 Demonstrates masterly of written language conventions 
 Uses words correctly with orthographic accuracy 
 Applies grammatical rules correctly in sentence construction 
 Punctuates different types of sentences correctly to convey meaning and clarity 
 Uses figurative language correctly 
 Writes continuous and non-continuous texts 
EoC 2 Interprets, summarises and translates continuous and non-continuous texts   
 Reads and understands various types of texts 
 Understands and uses words accurately and effectively, considering context, audience and 
purpose 
 Translates texts from a source language to a target language 
 Summarises texts in writing 
EoC 3 Interprets and understands spoken information 
 Identifies main ideas and supports details 
 Analyses speaker's purpose, tone and intent 
 Follows instructions, directions and questions and responds appropriately and correctly 
 Interprets texts from a source language to a target language accurately to convey the original 
message, tone and intent 
EoC 4 Expresses ideas, thoughts and opinions through spoken language 
 Presents thoughts and ideas logically 
 Speaks clearly and audibly 
 Uses appropriate vocabulary, expressions and tone for various purposes and audience 
3.  
Test Design 
The Zambian Languages examination will comprise two components. Component 1 will be School Based 
Assessment (SBA) which will be set and scored by teachers at school level from Form 2 to Form 3. 
Component 2 will be the Final Examination, centrally set by the Examinations Council of Zambia and 
administered at the end of Form 4.  
The weighting for SBA will be 30% and the Final Examination will be 70%.

3.1 
Structure 
3.1.1 
School Based Assessment- Component 1 
School Based Assessment will comprise a variety of assessment tasks whose structure will 
be determined by the type of task and by the teacher. The assessments will focus on the four 
language skills and will assess all the competences as outlined in all the four (4) Elements 
of Construct. The component will assess a total of fifteen (15) tasks; six (6) at Form 2 and 
nine (9) at Form 3 which will give a total of 200 marks.   
Learners in Form 3 will undertake one project of their choice in their target Zambian 
Language. The project is designed to strengthen advanced skills in writing, oral 
communication, research, creativity, and cultural appreciation. It will run across three 
school terms which are term 1, term 2 and term 3 with assessment conducted progressively. 
The final mark will be compiled at the end of Term 3 once the project is completed.  
3.1.2 
Final Examination: Component 2 
  The sections will have 5 items in total drawn from the two Elements of Construct as 
follows: 
           
            Section A - EoC 1: Demonstrates masterly of written language conventions 
Under the first section, candidates will be assessed on language structure. This section 
will have two scenarios each assessing five (5) structures. Each scenario will carry 
fifteen (15) marks making a total of thirty (30) marks for the whole section. Guidance on 
the correct answers will be provided in the draft scoring guide provided by the 
Examinations Council of Zambia. 
Section B - EoC 2:  Interprets, summarises and translates continuous and non-
continuous texts   
The section will have one scenario with ten (10) free response tasks under reading, 
carrying twenty (20) marks and a summary task carrying 10 marks, making a total mark 
of thirty (30) for the whole section. Guidance on the scoring will be provided as well. 
Section C - EoC 2: Interprets, summarises and translates continuous and non-
continuous texts   
The section will have one scenario with one task carrying fifteen (15) marks. The task 
will involve translating a given passage from a Zambian Language into English and vice

3.2 
Test Content 
The test content for both component 1 and component 2 will be considered in terms of item types, 
level of difficulty and syllabus coverage. The test content will be based on all the Elements of 
Construct. 
3.2.1 School Based Assessment: Component 1  
The test content under this component will cover topics from Form 1 to Form 3. However, 
topics for Form 1 will only be assessed in Form 2. School Based Assessment tasks will be 
based on all the Elements of Construct. Guidance on the tasks has been given in the School 
Based Assessment Guidelines for Examination for Certificate of Secondary School Ordinary 
Level - Literature and Languages.  
Types of School Based Assessment Tasks 
(i) 
The School Based Assessment will comprise both formative and summative assessment 
tasks. It will comprise a wide variety of form appropriate tasks covering all the four 
language skills. The SBA tasks will include: role-play, project, discussion, composition 
writing, short presentation, quiz, classwork, homework, summary writing, oral 
interviews, debate, language structure, reading comprehension, listening comprehension, 
reading fluency/reading aloud, storytelling, dictation, translation, speech, dialogue and 
tests (class tests and end of term tests).  
(ii) 
Note that all the tasks assessed in the Final Examination can also be assessed under SBA. 
(iii) 
All the tasks should be scenario based depicting a real-life situation. 
(iv) 
A variety of question types can be used. These may include: structured and semi-
structured questions (short answers, one word or sentence responses which can be used 

--- PDF_PAGE 108 ---

to assess language structure), subjective questions (short answers, essays/compositions, 
open ended and opinion-based) true or false, completion, matching and fill-in-the-blanks. 
 
Note:  Multiple choice items should be avoided at this level. This is because at secondary level, 
learners should be developing critical thinking, analysis, effective communication and 
problem-solving skills. They need to show the depth of their understanding and acquisition of 
skills which may be limited in multiple choice questions.  
3.2.2 Final Examination: Component 2  
The test content under this component will cover topics from Forms 1 to 4 of the Zambian 
Languages Syllabus – Secondary Education Ordinary Level Form 1-4. The component will 
assess competences from Elements of Construct 1 and 2 as specified under section 2.0. 
Types of Items  
The Final Examination will consist of the following questions/item types: semi- structured, 
short answers, open-ended and essay items. All these will be scenario-based. Candidates will 
be expected to provide short answers, define specific terms especially in comprehension 
passages, write detailed subjective responses like prose summary and translation as well as 
writing extended responses such as compositions that test creativity, critical thinking and 
writing skills. There will be no multiple-choice questions/tasks. 
Content Covered Under Each Section  
Section A: Language Structures 
This Section will be based on EoC 1 covering Language Structure. It will have two scenarios 
each assessing five structures. A total of ten (10) structures will be assessed. Candidates will be 
expected to answer tasks for both scenarios and each scenario will have tasks worth 15 marks. 
Section B: Reading Comprehension and Summary 
Section B will be based on EoC 2, covering reading comprehension and summary. The same 
comprehension passage will also be used to assess summary.  The length of the passage will be 
between 350 to 400 words. Candidates will be expected to respond to the summary item in 
prose form and within a given word limit. The reading comprehension part will have 20 marks 
while the summary part will have 10 marks. 
Section C: Translation  
This Section will be based on EoC 2, which will cover translation. It will have one item with 
165 to 170 words. This section will have 10 marks. 
Section D: Composition 
Section D will be based on EoC 1, covering composition writing. It will have one item which 
will have four composition tasks. Candidates will be expected to choose one. The section will 
have 30 marks. 
3.2.3    Level of Difficulty 
For both Component 1 which is School Based Assessment and component 2 which is the 
Final Examination, the tasks and test items will range from remembering to creating levels of 
the Bloom‘s taxonomy. All the tasks to be administered under SBA will need to be Form 
appropriate. However, for the Final Examination, content for all the Forms will be assessed. 
The levels of difficulty for both components will need to consider the following: 
(i) 
Complexity of scenario. The real-life situation on a given Element of Construct that a 
candidate will be expected to respond to correctly.

(ii) The subject knowledge and skills required to respond to the real-life problems and 
challenges correctly (level of competence).  
(iii) Cognitive level. The complexity and /or number of cognitive processes required to 
generate responses to test items and tasks.  
(iv) The level of abstract and critical thinking required to arrive at a response. 
(v) The extent to which a candidate will have to strategise in order to respond to the items or 
tasks.  
    3.2.4 
Syllabus Coverage 
The School Based Assessment and Final Examination will be based on the content from 
the Zambian Languages Syllabus Ordinary Level Secondary Education Form 1-4 (2024) 
developed by the Curriculum Development Centre. The SBA Framework, Test 
Framework and Test Specification will guide the development of the test items and tasks. 
The test items and tasks will be based on the competences grouped into Elements of 
Construct. The use of the frameworks and test specifications will ensure adequate 
syllabus coverage, validity, reliability, comparability and standardisation of the 
assessments from year to year.  
Both assessments will evaluate learners' progress in key and specific competences 
through the Elements of Construct.  The key competences include: analytical thinking, 
citizenship, collaboration, communication, creativity and innovation, critical thinking, 
emotional intelligence, environmental sustainability and problem-solving. These help to 
prepare candidates for real world challenges, foster informed decision-making and 
enhances employability as they transition into the world of work.

4. 
Scoring Criteria 
4.1 
The School Based Assessment will be scored by the teachers at school level. In Form 2 and 3, 
scores will be submitted yearly to the Examinations Council of Zambia. The Examinations Council 
of Zambia will add the scores for the two years and give a consolidated score that will contribute to 
the award of the final score. 
4.2   When scoring SBA, teachers should score out of 100 marks per Form. The weighting will be done by 
the Examinations Council of Zambia. 
4.3 
The Final Examination will be centrally scored by the Examinations Council of Zambia. 
4.4 
The scoring will be done by examiners trained by the Examinations Council of Zambia. 
4.5 
Before the scoring starts, coordination will be done in order to harmonise the scoring process by all 
the examiners. 
4.6 
Scoring will be done after the coordination. 
4.7 
In order to achieve an overall mark, candidates will be expected to sit both SBA tasks and the Final 
Examination. Learners who will not have SBA results will not be awarded a final score as ECZ does 
not process partial marks.  
5. 
Notes and Guidelines 
5.1 
Candidates will need to have results for both components in this examination for an overall score to 
be awarded. 
5.2 
The tasks should enable learners to acquire key and specific competences as outlined in the 
Zambian Languages Syllabus for Forms 1- 4. 
5.3 
SBA will be conducted in a flexible manner to enhance learning and acquisition of key and specific 
competences. 
5.4 
All the tasks to be scenario based. 
5.5  
The Final Examination will not assess individual topics or competences but selected Elements of 
Construct that were drawn from groups of competences.

3.2.1 
Types of Test Items  
              The assessment uses semi-structured and open-ended tasks.  
               Semi-structured tasks include restricted-response tasks, guided writing tasks, text 
transformation tasks, completion tasks, context-based writing tasks, guided performance 
tasks, text adaptation tasks, structured writing tasks, and convention-application tasks. 
These assess the application of literary features to create or adapt texts, speeches, or 
performances. 
               Open-ended tasks include extended writing tasks, authored work tasks with explanation, 
research-based project tasks, creative writing tasks, and performance tasks. These assess the 
production of literary or oral works and the explanation of artistic choices. 
               All task types require learner-produced text, speech, or performance and cover oral and 
written literature, poetry, novels, and drama.

Section A: Functional Literacy in Written and Oral Literature 
This focus area assesses learners‘ ability to apply knowledge of literary types, functions, 
and skills to create and use oral and written texts that educate, entertain, preserve culture, or 
generate entrepreneurial opportunities. 
Section B: Contextual (Poetry)  
This section assesses learners‘ skills in poetry, focusing on language proficiency, cultural 
competence, literary analysis, critical thinking, creative expression, and communication. 
Using a provided poetry text, learners analyse and interpret the language, tone, and style, 
and appreciate the cultural context. They identify poetic devices, interpret themes, and 
evaluate the poet's craft, demonstrating their understanding of Zambian poetry. This section 
is based on Element of Construct 2. 
Section C:  Contextual (Novel) 
This section requires learners to select two (2) texts from a list of five (5) prescribed texts. 
Learners will demonstrate their literary skills by analysing plot, characters, and themes, and 
appreciating the cultural context. They will also show understanding of the author's 
narrative techniques and literary devices, making connections to real-life situations. 
As they respond to the questions, learners demonstrate critical thinking and communicate 
their insights effectively in Zambian languages. The assessment evaluates their language 
proficiency, literary analysis, critical thinking, and communication skills. The section is 
based on Element of Construct 3. 
Section D:  Drama 
This section assesses learners' literary skills, critical thinking, and communication abilities 
in Zambian languages. Learners are expected to analyse the plot, characters, and themes of a 
provided drama text, appreciate the cultural context in which it is set, and understand the 
author's techniques. They are also required to relate their insights to real-life situations, 
demonstrating their ability to apply literary insights in a meaningful way. Learners will 
showcase their critical thinking, language proficiency, and literary analysis skills. The 
assessment provides a comprehensive evaluation of learners' understanding of the drama 
text and their ability to think critically about the text, communicate effectively, and 
appreciate Zambian cultural heritage. This section is based on Element of Construct  4. 
3.2.2 
Syllabus Coverage 
The School Based Assessment and Final Examination will be based on the Literature in

over time. Test Framework, SBA Framework and Test Specification will be used to ensure 
reliability and validity over time.  
3.2.3 
Level of Difficulty 
School-Based Assessment tasks and Final Examination test items are scenario-based, 
requiring learners to apply knowledge, attitudes, and skills to solve real-life problems. The 
scenario situation is based on a given element of construct that a candidate will be expected 
to respond to. The level of difficulty considers the following aspects: 
(i) 
Scenario complexity: Complexity of the real-life scenario 
(ii) 
Subject knowledge and skills: Required knowledge and skills to address the scenario

(iv)

(v)
