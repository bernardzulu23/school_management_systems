Chapter 16: Biology – 4012  
1. 
Construct 
Applies biological principles to real world situations to understand and solve challenges affecting living 
organisms. 
2. 
Elements of Construct 
Biology Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level shall be based on the 
following five (5) Elements of Construct (EoC). 
EoC1 
Analyses Systems of Classifying Organisms and Substances 
• Examines different classification systems. 
• Identifies features used for classification. 
• Evaluates the importance of classifying organisms and substances. 
EoC2 
Investigates and Explains Biological Functions 
• Investigates how living things function and interact. 
• Examines processes and patterns in organisms. 
• Analyses causes and effects of biological events. 
EoC3 
Interprets and Explains Scientific Evidence 
• Analyses data to draw scientific conclusions. 
• Interprets findings using logical scientific reasoning. 
• Evaluates evidence to support or reject ideas. 
EoC4 
Applies Scientific Skills to Real-life Situations 
• Uses scientific methods to solve everyday problems. 
• Applies practical skills in real-life investigations. 
• Makes informed decisions using scientific knowledge. 
 EoC 5 Demonstrates biological principles in real life situations 
• Applies biological knowledge to everyday experiences 
• Uses biology concepts to solve practical problems in daily life 
3. 
Test Design 
The Biology examination will be made up of two components namely component 1 and 2. Component 1 
will be the School Based Assessment and Component 2 is the Final Examination. 
School Based Assessment (SBA) - Component 1 will be set and administered by the school in Form 2 and 
3 while Component 2 will be centrally set by the Examinations Council of Zambia at the end of Form 4.  
The weighting of the School Based assessment and the Final Examination will be in a proportion of 30% 
to 70% respectively.

School Based Assessment will consist of 11 tasks that are completed across Form 2 and

Term two with a total mark of 50. The School Based Assessment will assess all the five (5)

level.

3.2 
Test content 
The test content of the Biology assessments is specified per assessment component, test item type, 
syllabus coverage and level of difficulty of the items. 
3.2.1 
 Types of Tasks and Items 
3.2.1.1 School Based Assessment - Component 1 
Tasks under School Based Assessment will comprise semi structured and essay 
type tasks and will be in the form of class tests, projects, practical activities and 
assignments. These tasks will be based on all the five (5) Elements of Construct. 
The tasks assessing experimental skills and investigations may be based on 
materials and specimen which may not be familiar to the learners. In responding to 
such tasks, learners will be required to follow instructions, make observations, 
record data, draw diagrams, make inferences or conclusions and apply concepts 
and principles as outlined in the syllabus. 
3.2.1.1 Final Examination - Component 2 
This will consist of semi-structured and essay type items. 
(i) 
Semi-structured items: These items will include a scenario presented as a 
real life problem, diagram, graph, table, picture, or a short stem, followed 
by a series of tasks. Candidates will be required to provide answers using 
words, numbers, symbols, phrases, definitions, comparisons, calculations, 
etc., in the spaces provided.

(ii) 
Essay type items: These items require candidates to answer in complete 
sentences and paragraphs, presenting their responses in a logical and 
coherent manner. They will also be originating from a scenario. 
3.2.2 
Level of Difficulty 
The level of difficulty of tasks in the School based Assessment and test items for the Final 
Examination will be governed by the following factors:  
(i) The subject knowledge and skills required to respond to the real-life problem and 
challenge correctly (level of competence); 
(ii) Complexity and/or number of cognitive processes required to generate responses to 
tasks and test items; 
(iii) The level of abstract and critical thinking involved; 
(iv) Extent to which the candidates have to strategise to respond to the tasks.; 
(v) The complexity of the real-life situation on a given Element of Construct a candidate 
will be expected to respond to correctly; 
 (iv)  the number of tasks the learner will be expected to respond to on a given real life    
situation. 
Some tasks and items may be based on unfamiliar materials. The expectation is that 
candidates should be able to apply scientific principles to generate responses to these types 
of tasks. To be successful at these tasks, candidates will need to draw on higher order 
thinking skills, including abstract and critical thinking through reasoning, analysing, 
creating and evaluating. Each of the two Biology assessment components will include tasks 
that will cover lower and higher order thinking skills.

3.2.3 
Syllabus Coverage 
The Biology examination will be based on the subject content in the Biology Syllabus 
Secondary Education Ordinary Level Form 1–4 (CDC 2024) and the competences outlined 
in the Competence Based Curriculum. Care must be taken to include cross-cutting issues 
such as HIV and AIDS, drug and substance abuse, climate change, gender and life skills 
based on Life Skills and Health Education (LSHE). Both assessment components will 
ensure full coverage of the syllabus by incorporating a cross representation of concepts, 
themes, and sub topics, while also assessing the Key and Specific Competences that 
learners are expected to develop. The assessment of Specific and Key Competences is 
important as this will: 
(i) help in aligning the curriculum with real life experiences and needs of learners, 
ensuring that education is relevant and practical; 
(ii) support the development of life long learning dispositions which are essential for 
preparing learners for future challenges and opportunities. 
In Component 2, a Test Specification has been developed to show the number of items that 
will be required to be set on each Element of Construct. This approach ensures that

4. 
Scoring Criteria 
The following scoring criteria will govern the evaluation of the Examination for Certificate of Secondary 
Education Ordinary Level Biology assessments for both School Based Assessment (SBA) and Final 
Examination. 
School-Based Assessment (SBA). 
Teachers will develop and use appropriate scoring guides or rubrics for each SBA task. These scoring 
guides will ensure consistency, fairness and accuracy when evaluating learners‘ performance in activities 
such as class tests, projects, practical work, field investigations, presentations, assignments, and other 
structured tasks.  
These scoring guides or rubrics will guide teachers in evaluating learners‘ performance in practical work, 
research, projects, assignments, presentations, and other structured activities to ensure consistency, 
fairness and accuracy when evaluating learner performance 
Final Examination 
The Final Examination will consist of specific scoring guides or rubrics. The scoring guides will be 
developed to support examiners in assessing learners‘ responses based on the required competences and 
Elements of Construct. These scoring guides will outline the key scientific skills, reasoning processes, and 
expected knowledge that demonstrate learner competence.  
Before marking begins, examiners will participate in a coordination meeting where acceptable alternative 
answers will be discussed to ensure that all valid responses are recognised. This process promotes 
consistency, fairness and accuracy in scoring.  
5. 
Notes and Guidelines 
This section highlights important notes and subject-specific guidelines that candidates and teachers must 
follow during the administration and scoring of the Biology assessments. These guidelines ensure fairness, 
consistency, and alignment with the competence-based Biology curriculum. 
 
5.1 
General Examination Guidelines 
(i) Candidates must sit for all components to be graded. 
(ii) All responses for the Final Examination must be written in the spaces provided within the 
question paper. 
(iii) Electronic devices and unauthorized materials are not allowed in the examination room. 
(iv) Time management is essential, especially in Final Examination, where structured and essay 
type items require organised and logical responses. 
 (v)  Non programmable calculators will be allowed in the examination room.  
5.2 
Biology-Specific Guidelines

(i) Diagrams must be large, neat, correctly labelled and drawn with a sharp HB pencil. Shading 
and colouring are not allowed unless instructed. 
(ii) When answering data-based items, candidates must show working steps, units and explanations 
where required. 
(iii) For tasks involving experiments or investigations, candidates must follow the scientific 
method, showing observations, reasoning and conclusions. 
(iv) Scientific terminologies must be used accurately. Vague and/or non-scientific statements will 
not earn full marks. 
(v) In items involving unfamiliar scenarios or materials, candidates must apply principles and 
concepts from the syllabus to make logical conclusions. 
(vi) Essay type items must be written in clear, coherent paragraphs with ideas presented logically to 
reflect higher order competences. 
6. 
Exemplar Test Items 
Exemplar items are sample test items provided to illustrate the type, format, and level of difficulty of 
questions that may appear in an assessment. They serve as a guide for both teachers and learners by 
showing how knowledge, skills, and competences can be assessed in practice. Exemplar questions 
demonstrate the alignment of assessment test items with specific competences and the desired cognitive 
levels, helping to ensure that teaching and evaluation are consistent with the curriculum. In a competency-
based framework, exemplar questions also show how learners are expected to apply knowledge and skills 
in practical, real-life situations, highlighting the specific competences targeted by each task.
