1. 
Construct  
Applies engineering principles and uses a wide range of tools and techniques to design and make a product 
that addresses a real-life problem. 
2. 
Elements of Construct  
Design and Technology Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level will be 
assessed under the four (4) Elements of Construct; 
EoC 1 
Utilises various materials and techniques to produce items while adhering to safety 
procedures 
• Utilises various materials, processes and safety measures to make products that respond to 
real life problems. 
EoC 2 
Generates and develops graphical representations and models 
• Interprets, generates and develops ideas in drawing, sketch or graphic form  
• Demonstrates creativity, resourcefulness and multi-skills in the Design Process 
EoC 3 
Designs and constructs innovative systems 
• Analyses, designs and constructs innovative systems anchored on System Technology 
EoC 4 
Develops and implements a business idea 
• Assesses candidates on entrepreneurship education and cross cutting issues.  
3. 
Test Design  
The Design and Technology (DT) assessment will consist of two components. Component 1 will be 
School Based Assessment (SBA) set and administered at school level from Form 2 to 3. Component 2 will 
be the Final Examination (FE) centrally set by Examinations Council of Zambia (ECZ) at the end of Form 
4. Candidates will be expected to attempt both components in order to be certified.

The test design will be considered in terms of structure and test content. 
3.1 
Structure 
Component 1 – Portfolio Production and Artefact Realisation 
Component 1 will assess using various tasks based on all the four (4) Elements of Construct. Each 
learner will undertake a project based on the Theme to be provided by the Examinations Council of 
Zambia. The Project will have two parts: Portfolio production and the artefact realisation.  
3.1.1 Portfolio Production - Part 1 
(i) 
Learners will be expected to produce a portfolio based on the theme. This will be 
assessed continuously by the subject teacher from the beginning of Form 2 to Form 3.  

--- PDF_PAGE 230 ---

(ii) 
All portfolios at this level will be compiled on A3 size paper. Details on the portfolio 
production and presentation will be supplied in the advance information.  
(iii) 
Each Form will tackle a theme that is indicating their final year. This is because more 
than one theme will be available in schools at one time. 
3.1.2 Artefact Realisation - Part 2 
(i) 
Learners will be expected to make an artefact each based on the chosen solution in 
their respective portfolios developed. 
(ii) 
A subject teacher is expected to guide candidates in applying the prescribed 
guidelines during the development and realisation of the artefacts. 
(iii) 
The subject teacher is also expected to assess and compile marks in readiness for 
submission when required. 
Component 2 – Theory and Design 
The test items will be theoretical and application-based in nature. It will assess candidates on all the 
four Elements of Construct. It will consist of two sections; In section A, two questions will be 
prepared based on EoC 1 and EoC 4 whilst Section B will consist of two questions on EoC 2 and 
EoC 3.  
In each test item, candidates will be expected to elicit the application of at least 3 of the following 
competences: Analytical Thinking, Creativity and Innovation, Critical Thinking, Problem Solving 
and Communication. 
Section A 
This section will consist of two compulsory test items; 
(i) 
Item 1 will be on materials, processes and safety. Candidates will be expected to elicit the 
application of at least 3 of the following competences: Analytical Thinking, Creativity and 
Innovation, Critical Thinking, Problem Solving and Communication. 
(ii) 
Item 2 will be on entrepreneurship. Candidates will be expected to highlight on how to 
develop and implement a business idea. They will also be expected to highlight one of the 
following cross-cutting issues; Environmental and Pollution Management, Climate Change or 
Education for Sustainable Development. 
Section B 
This section will consist of two test items; Item 3 and Item 4. 
Item 3 will be on System Technology. Candidates will be expected to design and construct 
innovative systems that can be used in real life situations. 
Item 4 will have two parts; Part I and Part II 
Part I – compulsory question 
Candidates will be expected to answer this part based on the Design Process.  
Part II – This part will be based on Graphic Communication.  
The part will consist of two items, one on Plane Geometry and the other on Solid Geometry. 
Candidates will be required to choose and attempt one item. 
3.2 
Test Content 
The test content for both Component 1 and 2 will be considered in terms of Test Item types, Level 
of Difficulty and Syllabus coverage. 
3.2.1 
Types of Tasks and Test Items 
Component 1 (SBA) will consist of both objective and subjective tasks which will include 
hands-on skills. The type of SBA tasks will also include minor projects, presentations,

3.2.2

competences acquired through knowledge and application of skills across all the six levels 
of the revised Bloom‘s taxonomy.

remembering to creating. Conceptual knowledge will be determined by the following 
factors: 
(i) 
Complexity of Scenario: The real-life situation on a given Element of Construct that a 
candidate will be expected to respond to correctly. 
(ii) 
The subject knowledge and skills required to the real-life problems and challenges 
correctly. 
(iii) 
The Cognitive Level- The complexity and number of cognitive processes required to  
generate responses to test items and tasks.

3.2.3 
Syllabus Coverage 
School Based Assessment and Final Examination will be based on the Design and 
Technology Studies syllabus for Form 1 to 4 produced by the Curriculum Development 
Centre, 2024.  It will also be based on the key competences such as analytical thinking, 
citizenship, collaboration, Communication, creativity and innovation, critical thinking, 
digital literacy, problem solving, entrepreneurship, financial Education and environmental 
sustainability.  
The tasks and test items will be based on related competences grouped together into 
Elements of Construct. Specific competences are reflected in the SBA Test Framework, Test 
Framework and Test Specification produced by the Examinations Council of Zambia. The 
Syllabus and these assessment tools will ensure, validity, reliability and comparability of 
the assessments from year to year. 
   
4. 
Scoring Criteria  
Component 1 (SBA): will be scored by teachers in their respective schools as guided in the scoring guides 
and rubrics in the School Based Assessment Guidelines for Examination for Certificate of Secondary 
Education Ordinary Level document provided by ECZ. 
Component 2 (FE): will be marked centrally by the ECZ using marking schemes provided by ECZ. (See 
Chapter 30 on Grading and Certification). 
To obtain an overall mark in Design and Technology, candidates must sit for both components. No overall 
grade will be awarded to any candidate who is absent from either component. 
5. 
Notes and Guidance  
The following requirements shall apply to all examination centres and candidates taking Design and 
Technology 
5.1 
All measurements will be in millimetres (mm) and that candidates shall be expected to be familiar 
with basic symbols and abbreviations found in the syllabus. 
5.2 
Candidates shall be expected to have a practical knowledge in Computer Aided Drawing (CAD) It 
will be used as an aid to generate simple engineering drawings.
