Chapter 18: Musical Arts – 5014  
1. 
Construct 
Demonstrates musical proficiency through analysing, composing and performing across diverse contexts. 
2. 
Elements of Construct 
Musical Arts examination will be based on five (5) Elements of Construct as follows: 
EoC1 
Analyses pitch  
 Analyses pitch in melodies and harmony 
EoC2 
Creates Melodies 
 Creates and analyzes melodies using the elements of music 
EoC3 
Arranges Musical Scores 
 Arranges music scores for musical instruments.  
EoC4 
Applies Musical Knowledge and Skills to Generate Income 
 Earns a living through musical skills.  
EoC5  
Analyses World Music 
 Characteristics of world music and how to use technology to produce it. 
3. 
Test Design 
The Musical Arts examination will consist of two assessments: Component 1 will be School Based 
Assessment (SBA) and Component 2 will be the Final Examination. Component 1 will be School Based 
Assessment (SBA) set by the subject teacher at school level in Form 2 and Form 3.  
Component 2 Composing, arranging and analysing: will be centrally set by the Examinations Council 
of Zambia at the end of Form 4. 
 
The weighting of the School Based assessment and the Final Examination will be in a proportion of 30% 
to 70% respectively.

3.2 Test content 
 Musical Arts content will cover the following areas; test item type, syllabus coverage and 
level of difficulty of the items, which will include Listening, sight reading, conducting, 
singing, composing project, dancing or playing a musical instrument leading to proficiency 
level. 
3.2.1 
Test Item Types 
Component 1 SBA [ listening, Performing and composing project]  
Will consist of tasks assessing aural skills which will be expressed through written 
responses. This component will assess expression of descriptive, discriminative/evaluative, 
structural listening, transcription, and performing skills. Composition project is affiliated to 
this component.  
Component 2 [composing, arranging and analysing] will focus on theoretical knowledge 
and understanding of principles in composing, arranging, harmonizing and analysing of 
music.  
3.2.2

from remembering to creating. Conceptual knowledge will be determined by the following

To define further the level of difficulty in sight-reading, the melodies will be based on C, F, 
G, D and B-flat major keys. The time signatures will be in the category of simple duple, 
simple triple and simple quadruple and compound duple, triple and quadruple. The 
melodies will be Four (4) measures long with the rhythmic frame based on the minim, 
crotchet, quaver, semiquaver, dotted crotchet and dotted quaver. The silences will be 
represented by the minim, crotchet and quaver rests. The range of these sight- reading 
melodies will be within an octave and should have a balanced combination of skips and 
steps. As indicated in the table above, this will assess the candidates‘ ability to interpret the 
melodies. 
3.2.3 
Syllabus Coverage 
The Ordinary level Musical Arts assessment will be based on the subject content in the 
syllabus by Curriculum Development Centre, 2024.  The two components will have tasks 
derived from all the main areas of musical skills as they appear in the syllabus and these are 
Analysing, Composing and Practical skills.

Scoring Criteria 
Component 1 – School Based Assessment (SBA) 
Performing and listening 
The marking of sight-reading will focus on rhythmic and melodic accuracy and the steadiness of pulse. It 
will be marked out of 10.  
The marking of dancing will focus on the appropriateness of style, expressiveness, use of space, 
symmetrical and asymmetrical use of the body and general impression. It will be marked out of 10. 
The marking of instrument playing will focus on tuning where applicable, tone quality where required, 
precision of keys played, rhythmic correctness, chord synchronization where necessary, and general 
impression. It will have 10 marks. 
The marking of the singing skill will focus on posture, voice production, diction and interpretation. It will 
be marked out of 10.

3.2.2  
Level of Difficulty 
The level of difficulty of test items in Components 1 and 2 will assess the level of 
competences acquired through knowledge and application of skills across all the six levels 
of the revised Bloom‘s taxonomy.  
The level of difficulty of tasks in both SBA tasks and Final Examination will range from 
remembering to creating. Conceptual knowledge will be determined by the following 
factors: 
(i) 
Complexity of Scenario: The real-life situation on a given Element of Construct that a 
candidate will be expected to respond to correctly. 
(ii) 
The subject knowledge and skills required to the real-life problems and challenges 
correctly. 
(iii) 
The Cognitive Level- The complexity and number of cognitive processes required to  
generate responses to test items and tasks.

3.2.3 
Syllabus Coverage 
School Based Assessment and Final Examination will be based on the Design and 
Technology Studies syllabus for Form 1 to 4 produced by the Curriculum Development 
Centre, 2024.  It will also be based on the key competences such as analytical thinking, 
citizenship, collaboration, Communication, creativity and innovation, critical thinking, 
digital literacy, problem solving, entrepreneurship, financial Education and environmental 
sustainability.  
The tasks and test items will be based on related competences grouped together into 
Elements of Construct. Specific competences are reflected in the SBA Test Framework, Test 
Framework and Test Specification produced by the Examinations Council of Zambia. The 
Syllabus and these assessment tools will ensure, validity, reliability and comparability of 
the assessments from year to year. 
   
4. 
Scoring Criteria  
Component 1 (SBA): will be scored by teachers in their respective schools as guided in the scoring guides 
and rubrics in the School Based Assessment Guidelines for Examination for Certificate of Secondary 
Education Ordinary Level document provided by ECZ. 
Component 2 (FE): will be marked centrally by the ECZ using marking schemes provided by ECZ. (See 
Chapter 30 on Grading and Certification). 
To obtain an overall mark in Design and Technology, candidates must sit for both components. No overall 
grade will be awarded to any candidate who is absent from either component. 
5. 
Notes and Guidance  
The following requirements shall apply to all examination centres and candidates taking Design and 
Technology 
5.1 
All measurements will be in millimetres (mm) and that candidates shall be expected to be familiar 
with basic symbols and abbreviations found in the syllabus. 
5.2 
Candidates shall be expected to have a practical knowledge in Computer Aided Drawing (CAD) It 
will be used as an aid to generate simple engineering drawings.
