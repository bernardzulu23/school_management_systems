Chapter 5: History – 3013  
1. 
Construct  
Demonstrates understanding of how historical(past) events and current trends shape the future.   
2. 
Elements of Construct 
The History examination will be based on the following five (5) Elements of Construct (EoC): 
EoC1 
Demonstrates understanding of ideologies of Evolution and early civilisation 
 Evaluates understanding of Evolution and early civilisation. 
 Demonstrates society‘s core values, beliefs, morals and principles in real life    situation 
EoC2 
Appreciates historical sites and traditional practices  
 Assesses knowledge of Zambia‘s historical sites and cultural roots. 
EoC3 
Exhibits good morals, principles and national values 
 Demonstrates core values, beliefs, morals and customs 
EoC4 
Promotes national patriotism and citizenship through historical events while fostering 
collaboration with local, regional and international communities 
 Engages communities in historical and patriotic activities to enhance good citizenry and 
collaborative understanding of national issues  
EoC5 
Analyses emerging global issues and their sustainability 
 Demonstrates understanding of contemporary issues and their effects to the environment 
3.  
Test Design  
The History Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level and General Certificate 
Examinations will have two components as follows: 
Component 1: School Based Assessment (SBA) This will be locally set and administered by teachers in 
respective schools, following the School Based Assessment Guidelines for the Examination for Secondary 
Education Ordinary Level for Social Sciences provided by the Examinations Council of Zambia. SBA will 
be administered in Forms 2 and 3. SBA will contribute 30% towards the final assessment. 
Component 2:  Final Examination (Theory Paper). This will be centrally set by the Examinations Council 
of Zambia and administered in Form 4. The Final Examination will contribute 70% towards the final 
score.

Structure

 The structure of the SBA tasks will be determined by the subject teacher. This will consist of 
all types of items in form of class tests, debates, assignments projects, group discussions, 
presentations, role play and assignments. There will be a specific project as part of the SBA. 
All the SBA tasks should be based all the five Elements of Constructs order to adequately 
assess learners in the specific competences. 
 
3.1.2 Final Examination Component 2  
The Final Examination will have two Sections; Section A and B. 
 
(i) 
Section A  
This section will comprise three (3) items drawn from three (EoCs) 1,2, and 3 
Candidates will be expected to attempt all the three items. Each item will carry 20 
marks and will be based on real -life situation presented using any of the following: 
narrations, maps, diagrams, graphs, charts, illustrations, tables, statistical data, pictures 
or any other relevant source.  
(ii) 
Section B  
This section will comprise four (4) items drawn from two (EoCs), 4 and 5. Candidates 
will be expected to attempt any two (2) items. Each item will carry 20 marks. 
Candidates will be required to write an essay based on a real-life situation scenario.

assessments. The teacher will decide to present the tasks structured, semi structured or 
unstructured. will comprise objective and subjective tasks from real life situations. The 
tasks will include assignments, presentations, class exercises, practical activities, quizzes 
and tests by individual learners or in groups. SBA tasks will include a project that is 
subject specific. (For details, refer to the School Based Assessment Guidelines for

3.3.2  Final Examination - Component 2 will consist of six (6) scenarios each with several 
tasks and candidates will answer tasks on five different scenarios. Each scenario will 
have tasks that will have a total of 20 marks giving a total of 100 marks. The six 
scenarios will come from the five EoC and the tasks that will follow will be based on the 
scenarios provided. Candidates will apply the key and specific competences in terms of 
knowledge and skills acquired at this level in order to find solutions to the problems.    
3.4.  Syllabus Coverage 
The History examination will be based on the 2024 History Syllabus for Secondary Education 
Ordinary Level, form 1-4, developed by Curriculum Development Centre (CDC, 2024) as well as 
the Test Specification, Test Framework and SBA Framework developed by the Examinations 
Council of Zambia should be used during the development of SBA tasks and Final Examination test 
items to ensure validity, reliability and comparability of the examination from year to year. 
The SBA tasks and test items for the Final Examination will be based on key and specific 
competences, which are clustered according to their relatedness into Elements of Constructs (EoCs). 
3.5  
Level of Difficulty 
The level of difficult for History School Based Assessment (SBA) and Final Examinations items 
will range from remembering to creating levels of revised Bloom‘s taxonomy. 
The level of difficulty of the items will be determined by the following factors: 
(i) 
The real-life situation linked to a given Element of Construct which the learner will be 
expected to respond to. 
(ii) 
The subject knowledge required to address real-life problem-solving situation. 
(iii) 
The complexity and number of cognitive processes required to generate responses. 
(iv) 
The level of knowledge, skills, values and attitudes applied to respond correctly. 
(v) 
The strategies employed in the process of answering questions.

(ii) 
During the scoring exercise, team leaders will continue coordinating the work of the 
examiners to ensure standards are maintained. 
(iii) 
Candidates will be assessed for coherent understanding, application, analysis, evaluation and 
creation in interpreting scenarios given in tasks and real-life contexts.  
 (iv)  Extended responses should provide analysis and interpretation of real-life scenarios, applying 
skills to solve existing problems.

Notes and Guidelines 
5.1 
School Based Assessment-Component 1

Final Examination –Component 2  
(i) 
All SBA marks should be marked out of 100 per year and raw marks submitted to ECZ. 
(ii) 
Candidates must sit for both components in order to be graded. ECZ does not process partial 
scores 
(iii) 
The overall scores shall be awarded to the candidates who successfully attempts both 
components of assessments 
(iv) 
Calculators, electronic devices and unauthorised materials are not allowed in the examination 
room or during the administration of SBA 
(v) 
Text books and other reference materials are also not allowed during the examination. 
(vi) 
All questions will be answered in the spaces provided in the question paper. 
(vii) Extended responses must be written in paragraphs and in full sentences (not point form)   
(viii) Extended responses should contain relevant information and appropriate analysis of the 
scenarios presented in order to score marks.
