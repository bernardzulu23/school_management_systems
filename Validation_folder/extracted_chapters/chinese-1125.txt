Chapter 8: Chinese Language – 1125  
1 
Construct 
Demonstrates proficient use of listening, speaking, reading and writing skills in real-life situations, 
adapting to audience and purpose to communicate effectively and support life-long learning. 
2 
Elements of Construct 
Assessment of candidates will be based on the following four (4) Elements of Construct (EoC):  
EoC1 
Reads, interprets, summarises and translates continuous and non-continuous texts 
 Reads and comprehends various types of continuous and non-continuous texts. 
 Interprets vocabulary and expressions accurately, considering context, audience and purpose. 
 Identifies and summarises main ideas and supporting details. 
 Translates texts accurately from the source language into the target language, preserving 
meaning, tone and intent.  
EoC2 
Demonstrates mastery of written language conventions and transformations 
 Spells words correctly with orthographic accuracy. 
 Applies grammatical and syntactic rules accurately in sentence construction. 
 Uses appropriate punctuation to enhance clarity and meaning. 
 Manipulates and transforms written language structures while maintaining grammatical 
accuracy and meaning. 
EoC3     Understands and interprets spoken information 
 Identifies main ideas and supporting details in spoken texts. 
 Analyses the speaker‘s purpose, tone and intended message. 
 Follows instructions, directions and questions accurately and responds appropriately.  
 Interprets spoken information from source language into the target language while 
maintaining meaning and intent.  
EoC 4 
Produces ideas, thoughts and opinions through spoken language 
 Organises ideas logically and coherently. 
 Speaks clearly, audibly and fluently. 
 Uses appropriate vocabulary, expressions and tone suited to purpose, context and audience.  
 Demonstrates accuracy in pronunciation and grammatical structures.  
3 
Test Design 
The Chinese Language Examination for School Certificate of Education Ordinary Level will consist of 
two components: Component 1, School Based Assessment (SBA), and Component 2, the Final 
Examination. The weighting of the School Based Assessment (SBA) and the Final Examination will be in 
the ratio of 40:60.

3.1 
Structure 
3.1.1 
School Based Assessment (SBA) - Component 1 
School-Based Assessment (SBA) shall be conducted from Form 2 to Form 3 and shall 
constitute an integral component of the teaching and learning process.     
SBA tasks will be drawn from all the four (4) Elements of Construct and shall   include 
classwork, homework, assignments, oral presentations and projects.  
3.1.2 
Final Examination - Component 2 
The Final Examination will be based on Elements of Construct, namely, EoC 1: Reads, 
Interprets, summarises and translates continuous and non-continuous texts and EoC 2: 
Demonstrates mastery of written language conventions and transformations. The paper will 
comprise two sections A and B. 
3.1.3 
Section A: Reading Comprehension  
Reading Comprehension will comprise 2 items which will have 2 scenarios in form of 
passages. The passages will be non-continuous and continuous passages. Candidates will 
be expected to answer both.   
Passages used in the examination will be adapted to the candidates‘ level of competence as 
prescribed in the syllabus. Candidates will be required to read texts written in Chinese 
Language and respond to questions based on the given scenarios. Each continuous text will 
include semi-structured tasks, weighted at 20 marks. 
Non-continuous texts will be organised in a non-linear manner, with information presented 
in the form of timetables, maps, diagrams, picture series, and similar formats. The visual 
presentations will contain sufficient information to elicit responses and will include semi-
structured tasks, weighted at 20 marks. 
3.1.4 
Section B: Writing  
This section will comprise two parts: guided and unguided composition. In the guided 
composition part, candidates will attempt one compulsory item. In the unguided 
composition part, candidates shall be presented with two items and shall be required to 
choose one. 
Both the guided and unguided compositions will each carry a total of 10 marks. 
Writing tasks will be based on the following types of compositions:  
(i) Informal and formal letter writing 
(ii) Narrative writing 
(iii) Descriptive writing 
(iv) Argumentative writing 
(v) Curriculum vitae writing 
(vi) Profile writing 
3.2 
Test Content 
The SBA at Form 2 and 3 will assess all competences outlined in the syllabus at either form level. 
The Final Examination will include topics from the syllabus from Forms 1 to 4. 
3.2.1 
Types of Tasks and Items

            Items in both School Based Assessment (SBA) and the Final Examination shall comprise a 
combination of objective and subjective types. 
(i) The item types to be used in the School Based Assessment (SBA) shall include 
multiple-choice tasks, true or false items, matching items, short response questions, 
essay questions, and short answer questions, among others. 
(ii) The Final Examination shall comprise structured and semi-structured item types, 
including short response questions, completion of sentences, and essay items. 
3.2.2 
 Level of Difficulty 
             The level of questions in both the Final Examination and School Based Assessment (SBA) 
of the Chinese Language will be based on the Elements of Construct and will vary in 
complexity to ensure balanced coverage across lower-order, middle-order and higher-order 
cognitive domain of the revised Bloom‘s Taxonomy.  
              Assessment items shall be scenario-based and designed to require candidates to apply their 
knowledge, skills, values and attitudes to solve real-life communicative problems. 
              The level of difficulty of the Final Examination and SBA items shall be determined by the 
following factors: 
(i) The depth and breadth of the subject knowledge and language skills required to address 
the given scenario. 
(ii) The level of cognitive complexity involved in processing and responding to the task. 
(iii) The degree of abstract reasoning and critical thinking required.  
(iv) The number and complexity of cognitive processes necessary to generate appropriate 
and meaningful response.  
3.2.3 
Syllabus Coverage 
The Final Examination and School-Based Assessment (SBA) shall be based on the Chinese 
Language Syllabus (CDC,2024) for Forms 1 to 4. The assessment shall focus on the 
development and evaluation of key competences, such as communication, analytical 
thinking, citizenship, collaboration, problem-solving and critical thinking.  
Assessment tasks shall be designed to integrate these competences within authentic 
language contexts to promote meaningful learning and practical application. To ensure 
validity, reliability, consistency and comparability across examination cycles, the Test 
Specification, Test Framework and School Based Assessment (SBA) Framework shall be 
applied. These tools shall guide and standardise development and moderation of test items 
and SBA tasks.

4. 
Scoring Criteria 
4.1  
School Based Assessment – Component 1 Scoring Criteria 
SBA tasks will be prepared and scored by the subject teacher at the school. To ensure tasks are 
pitched at the appropriate level of difficulty, teachers will use the SBA Framework.  
Teachers will use the Scoring Guides for each SBA task to ensure consistency, fairness, and 
accuracy when evaluating learners‘ performance on class tests, projects, practical work, 
presentations, assignments, and other structured activities.

4.2 
Final Examination – Component 2 
Before scoring of the Final Examination commences, a co-ordination meeting will be held to 
harmonise the Scoring Guide for that particular year. Furthermore, during marking, continuous 
checking of scoring will be done to ensure that examiners maintain the standard of scoring by 
conforming to the Scoring Guide.  
5. 
Notes and Guidelines 
5.1 
Candidates will be expected to sit for both Component 1 and Component 2 in order to be graded 
in the subject. If a candidate absconds from one of the Components, no partial marks shall be 
awarded. 
5.2 
Standard Chinese shall be used in answering examination questions.  
5.3 
Dictionaries, textbooks and any other reference books will not be allowed in the examination room. 
5.4 
Electronic devices such as mobile phones, tablets, iPods and laptops will not be allowed in the 
examination room. 
Note: Detailed guidelines for School-Based Assessment are stipulated in the „Guidelines for School Based 
Assessment‟ developed by the Examinations Council of Zambia.
