Construct 
Demonstrates strong commercial competences, entrepreneurial skills and ethical values for personal 
empowerment and effective participation in economic activities and national development. 
2 
Elements of Construct 
The Commerce examination will be based on the four (4) Elements of Construct and they will be as 
follows: 
EoC 1 
Demonstrates the skills of clean production and trading within legal parameters 
• 
Demonstrates conceptual understanding of commerce and career prospects 
 
Demonstrates understanding of the interaction of commerce, production, services and 
specialisation to provide efficient human satisfaction and promote economic growth. 
 
Demonstrates the understanding of factors of production 
 
Practises safe environmental management strategies, sustainable and socially responsible 
conduct of commerce and production processes. 
 
Demonstrates understanding of buying and selling on credit. 
 
Demonstrates understanding of contracts, trade laws and restrictions and exhibit effective 
legal compliance. 
 
Demonstrates effective skills and applies innovative strategies in efficient organisation and 
management of domestic and international businesses.  
 
Demonstrates proper documentation, procedural and ethical compliance in the conduct of 
domestic and international businesses. 
EoC 2 
Demonstrates skills in entrepreneurship, Business formation and financing 
• Demonstrates effective entrepreneurial skills for wealth creation and solve personal and real-
world distribution and economic problems 
• Demonstrates financial safety management and analyse sourcing options to appropriately 
solve capital and liquidity challenges in business 
• Demonstrates knowledge of business formation procedures and effectively perform business 
operational analysis  
• Performs entrepreneurial activities and practice actualization of business ideas into viable 
businesses

Applies physical and Online Business practices free from exploitation 
• Demonstrates effective digital entrepreneurship and practises E-Commerce safely 
• Demonstrates understanding of online and physical consumer protection mechanisms void of 
cyber and physical exploitative tendencies 
• Demonstrates ethical and transparent standards and applies legal personal and commercial 
data privacy practices appropriately. 
• Exhibits understanding of proper management of online payment systems  
EoC 4 
Analyses and evaluates the significance of commercial services in production, commerce 
and direct service provision 
• Demonstrates the critical role of commercial services in non-commercial activities and drives 
success in the real wider business world. 
• Evaluates significance of commercial services in production, commerce and direct services.

3.1. 
Structure 
The structure shall comprise of SBA and Final Examination (FE) as follows; 
3.1.1 School Based Assessment - Component 1 
SBA will be assessed by the teacher in Form 2 and Form 3 only, using different types of tasks.  
A total of twelve (12) SBA tasks will be administered, six (6) of them at each of the 
prescribed two Form Levels. The task will comprise of the scenario and subsequent task 
items or questions which may also have other subsequent sub scenarios as detailed in the test 
design table.  
The structure and type of each SBA task will be determined by the teacher at school level and 
guided by the School Based Assessment Guidelines for the Examination for Certificate of 
Secondary Education Ordinary Level for Business Studies.   
                    3.1.2 Final Examination - Component 2 
The Final Examination (FE) Component 2 will be centrally set by the Examinations Council 
of Zambia (ECZ) and will be administered in Form 4 at the end of term 3 according to the 
Final Examination time table. 
The Final Commerce Examination Paper will consist of two sections namely Section A and 
Section B.  
Section A will have three (3) compulsory items or questions based on EoC 1, EoC 2 and EoC 
3. The three (3) items or questions will carry a total of 65 marks for Section A. 
Section B will have two (2) optional items both from EoC 4 from which candidates will 
choose any one (1) item. Both items will carry 35 marks each.  
Each item will have tasks which will be restricted to not more than five (5) per item. 
Further, all tasks in both sections will be attached to the scenario. A maximum of two (2) 
scenarios will be acceptable per item.  Additional brief sub scenarios up to maximum of four 
(4) may be attached to the tasks.

3.2 
 
Test Content 
The Commerce Ordinary Level Assessments will assess all the competences outlined in the 2024 
Competence Based syllabus for Commerce, produced by Curriculum   Development Centre. 
Further, also on the Elements of Construct and all the competences in the test and specification 
frameworks developed by the Examinations Council of Zambia (ECZ).  
      3.2.1  Item Types 
 3.2.1.1  
SBA - Component 1 
SBA Component 1 will consist of structural formative and summative items 
in form   of class exercises, class tests, discussions, debates, assignments, 
presentations, case studies, excursions or field trips project reports, weekly 
tests, end of topic tests, mid-term tests, end of term tests and end of year tests. 
The types of tasks have been categorised into the three major assessment task 
groupings namely; theory, application and investigative tasks. Hands on 
commercial activities and practical requirements are emphasized and 
encouraged for learners such as generating of business plans, participating in 
tuck shops among other practicable activities in Commerce. 
 3.2.1.2  
Final Examination - Component 2  
The Final Examination (FE) will consist of subjective item types where 
candidates will provide short and extended responses of both short and long 
essay type which will be dependent on the item task requirements. The 
responses should be written in point form.

the   following main factors among others:  
 (i)

3.2.3 
Syllabus Coverage 
The Commerce teaching syllabus for 2024 by CDC is the policy document that provides the 
basis for the development of the examination. The Test Framework, Test Specification and SBA 
Framework developed by ECZ will guide the development of Commerce examination. Both 
SBA and Final Examination will cover the whole syllabus assessing all the key and specific 
competences outlined in these documents. The assessment will measure the extent to which 
candidates will apply the acquired competences in the real world of business for personal and 
national development. Both SBA and Final Examination will be based on the four (4) Elements 
of Construct developed by the ECZ from the syllabus.  
3.2.1  SBA - Component 1 
SBA will cover the whole syllabus and will be based on the School Based Assessment 
Guidelines for the Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education Ordinary Level 
for Business Studies which contain the SBA Framework that guide Commerce teachers 
during the development of SBA tasks. It will assess all the competences and ensure 
holistic lifelong learning through effective balance among the three domains of 
cognitive (mental knowledge acquired), psychomotor (hands on commercial skills) and 
affective (emotions, values and attitudes) to real life beyond classroom. Further the 
component will endeavour to promote the practical context and competences 
achievement of learners to address personal and societal needs and everyday situations. 
3.2.2  Final Examination - Component 2 
The Final Examination will also cover the entire syllabus. It will be based on the Test 
Specification and Test Framework. The Final Examination will be based on the four 
Elements of Construct and will cover the key and specific competences. The Final

4         Scoring Criteria 
The Commerce scoring criteria will ensure that every mark awarded reflects the extent and measure of 
what a learner can do, create, and sustain in the real world of business. It measures learner‘s ability to 
demonstrate knowledge mastery, apply intelligence through creativity and innovation, demonstrate 
practical skill, think critically, and uphold ethical values. The two components SBA and Final 
Examination (FE) will be assessed and scored as follows: 
4.1 
SBA Component 1- SBA will be assessed and scored at school level by the teacher using the 
School Based Assessment Guidelines for the Examination for Certificate of Secondary Education 
Ordinary Level for Business Studies provided by the ECZ. The teacher should adhere to the 
provided guidelines to appropriately allocate, score and award SBA marks to learners. The SBA 
marks for each of the two (2) years thus Form 2 and Form 3 will be submitted to the ECZ.  The 
teacher will submit the scores in raw form only. ECZ will do the required computation of weightage 
to determine the weighted score for each learner. 
4.2 
Component 2 – Final Examination (FE) will be centrally set and marked by the ECZ. It will be 
marked out of 100 marks. The Final Examination Marking Coordination Meeting will be conducted 
with both Team Leaders and Examiners before actual marking begins. This will harmonise the 
scoring guide and ensure marking scheme is coordinated, Examiners are familiarised in order to 
fully equip them to appropriately score candidates.  Continuous monitoring during marking by 
various stakeholders will also be done to ensure consistency in scoring and award of marks.

SOLUTION FOR ITEM 5 
(a)  
(i)

(ii)  
importance 
− Risk Mitigation: Replaces the buyer's credit risk with the bank's, protecting sellers from non-payment 
and buyers from non-delivery. 
− Payment Guarantee: The bank guarantees payment to the seller once compliant documents (like bills 
of lading, invoices) are presented, ensuring the seller gets paid after shipping. 
− Financing Tool: Allows exporters to get pre-shipment (packing credit) or post-shipment financing, as 
the DC serves as collateral. 
− Working Capital: Buyers can improve cash flow as payment is made after shipment, potentially 
securing longer credit terms. 
− Intermediary Role: Banks act as trusted intermediaries, so buyer and seller don't need direct 
communication for payment assurance. 
− Structured Compliance: Ensures both parties adhere to strict terms and conditions, essential for 
complex government or large-scale trade 
(b) − 
Immediate Access to Funds: The seller receives cash immediately from a financial institution (minus a discount 
fee/interest charge), rather than having to wait until the bill's maturity date. This provides immediate working

capital. 
− Improved Cash Flow and Liquidity: Access to quick funds allows the seller to manage day-to-day operations, 
cover expenses, reinvest in the business, and take on new orders without being constrained by the buyer's 
payment cycle. 
− Risk Mitigation (Optional): In "without recourse" discounting, the financial institution assumes the risk of the 
buyer defaulting on payment. The seller is protected from potential losses. 
− Enhanced Negotiation Power: The seller can offer competitive or extended payment terms to the buyer (e.g., 90 
days credit) without negatively impacting their own cash flow, which can strengthen business relationships and 
attract more customers. 
− Off-Balance-Sheet Financing: In some cases, bill discounting does not count as a loan, so it does not increase 
the seller's debt on their balance sheet.
